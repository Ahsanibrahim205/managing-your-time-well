Why managing your time
well will improve your wellness

  • Research evidence suggests that much of the psychological turmoil you experience in daily living have its origin in the time pressures that you impose on yourself.
  • Time is one of the most precious commodities that you have, and you should use it as if it were a limited reserve. In today's society rushing and hectic lifestyles are commonplace.
  • If for you ‘time is money’ and little else, it can lead you to an obsessive preoccupation with filling your time; getting in as many activities as possible in as short a time as possible. At that point you have lost the meaning of time.
  • The consequences are that your daily life can be dictated by tight and complex time-schedules. This fixation on time serves ironically to displace you from the present, focus excessively on the future and deny us the pleasure of experiencing ‘authentic living’.
  • How can you learn to free yourself from these self-imposed time constrictions? In these circumstances it is crucial that techniques are learned for slowing down your personal life tempo. This will improve your wellbeing. The advice below consists of 3 sections: general improvements, improvements at work and improvements at home.

    • By choosing to follow one or more of these pieces of advice you will eventually develop a habit which will help you maintain your well-being even though the pace of life is relentlessly increasing and is likely to continue to do so in the foreseeable future.

    Managing your time well
    general improvements

  • One of the most obvious ways of doing this would be by getting into the routine of taking a whole day off per week, without any commitment to work at all. This would open up time to choose what to do, or to do nothing.
  • You could re-learn the pleasures of spontaneous play, watching and participating in games and recreational activities. Playing is a good territory for practising skills in moderating one’s pace of life.
  • An equally effective and quite different leisure activity with similar effects to those already mentioned would be relaxing by listening to different types of music. Select music of different patterns and tempo that can help the process of changing your personal attitude towards tempo. Learn over a period of time to appreciate slow as well as fast, calm as well as wild music.
  • If you become aware that you are pacing quickly to reach a destination, pause, take a breath, reduce your stride by one half, and adopt a slow rhythm of movement. To help you to walk at a slower pace you can listen to easy paced music or a tune in your head
  • You can learn to play with time by consciously dawdling, and deliberately performing more movements in slow motion. To become aware of your pace during the day for example, get into the habit of stopping yourself, perhaps at specific times of the day, and making a subjective estimate of the time and pace of your day. Ask yourself “How fast is my internal clock running on a scale from 1- 10”? As you monitor your pace in this way your awareness will help you to start to slow your pace down.
  • Try choosing to speak less and listening more than usual. Become aware of any tendency you have to interrupt others and finish their sentences for them. Cut down on this time pressured habit.
